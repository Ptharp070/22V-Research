The 22V Entry Philosophy

How Visser & DeBusschere Think About Timing

22V doesn't chase. Their framework is: identify structural themes with multi-year runways, then use market volatility as your entry mechanism. The thesis doesn't change on a 10% pullback — the price does. The key distinction is between positions where the thesis is structural (buy any meaningful dip) vs. tactical (wait for a specific catalyst).

Jordi Visser — Entry Rules
Seller on rallies for the broad US market. Buyer on pullbacks for AI buildout, commodities, and scarcity names. The themes haven't changed — only sentiment has. Add to and build positions on recent pullbacks. Don't wait for the perfect bottom.
"He is supportive of stepping into well-positioned AI build-out levered names that have sold off, as those themes haven't changed."
Dennis DeBusschere — Entry Rules
Uses Equity Risk Premium (ERP) as the macro timing signal. ERP at ~5% = historically consistent with 1970s stagflation. "Difficult short but not yet an obvious buy." Volatility spikes driven by AI uncertainty are buying opportunities. Favor companies with physical assets — harder to disrupt.
"Rising AI disruption risk and concern that financial conditions may need to tighten are driving rotation toward asset-heavy, cash-returning sectors. This is a margin-of-safety trade, not a recession call."

AI BUILDOUT Hyperscaler capex plans accelerating, not decelerating. "Concerns that AI capex is being pulled forward are premature." Structural demand intact for power, compute, memory, networking.

SCARCITY Physical-world assets outperforming software. Equal-weight semis up 65% since May lows while software down 5%. Memory, silver, energy in structural shortage.

GEOPOLITICAL Iran moving toward de-escalation but lasting instability in energy markets. Oil structurally elevated. Defense spending $200B+ likely.

MAG7 Mega-cap tech in "unified breakdown." S&P 500 difficult short but not obvious buy. Leadership vacuum makes sustained rallies unlikely near-term.

Bottom line: This is NOT a "buy everything now" market. It IS a "buy the RIGHT things on pullbacks" market. Be selective. Be patient. The scarcity trade is working — deploy capital there first.

Position-by-Position Entry Guide

Tier 1 — Build First
CEG Constellation Energy · AI Power / Nuclear
12% target
The AI data center power bottleneck play. 22V identifies power delivery as the new constraint replacing GPUs. Constellation's nuclear fleet provides baseload clean energy that AI data centers need 24/7. Calpine acquisition expands capacity. Every persona in the debate wants this name.
Entry Trigger
Buy on any 8-12% pullback from recent levels. Current ~$270 range is already well off the $413 52-week high (~35% drawdown). The thesis strengthens as AI capex accelerates. New data center contracts are the upside catalyst.
Pullback Levels
First tranche: current levels ($265-280). Second tranche: $240-250 (near 2026 lows). Third tranche: $200-220 (if broader market sells off). 52-wk low: $161.
Risk Signal
No new data center deals announced at recent investor day. PJM capacity market regulatory changes. Nuclear regulatory headwinds. If hyperscaler capex guidance weakens in earnings.
Next Catalyst
Earnings May 7, 2026. Watch for new data center power purchase agreements. PJM capacity auction results. Calpine integration updates.
AVGO Broadcom · AI Compute / Networking
11% target
The "whole rack" networking play. Visser's thesis is that AI infrastructure demand is expanding from GPUs to the entire data center stack — CPUs, networking, storage. Broadcom dominates custom AI accelerators (ASICs) and ultra-high-speed networking (Tomahawk switches). Revenue up 29% YoY, AI semiconductor revenue up 106% YoY.
Entry Trigger
Buy on market-wide selloffs, not AVGO-specific weakness. AI semis sell off with the index but the structural demand is unchanged. TSMC capacity fully stretched = supply constraint validates demand. Current ~$330 range.
Pullback Levels
First tranche: $310-330. Second tranche: $280-300 (meaningful pullback). Third tranche: $250 (extreme — would be a gift). 52-wk range: low ~$113, high ~$415.
Risk Signal
Non-AI business weakness (VMware integration, broadband). Hyperscaler ASIC program cancellations. TSMC supply disruption (Taiwan risk). Earnings miss on the infrastructure software segment.
Next Catalyst
Earnings June 4, 2026. Watch hyperscaler capex guidance (MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN earnings in late April). New ASIC design wins. Tomahawk 6 adoption.
VST Vistra · AI Power
9% target
Second power play alongside CEG. Vistra operates natural gas and nuclear generation in ERCOT (Texas) and PJM. Benefits from the same AI data center power demand thesis. Texas is a primary data center build-out geography. More levered to natural gas prices than CEG.
Entry Trigger
Buy alongside CEG on power sector pullbacks. These two move together. VST offers more upside leverage but also more gas-price sensitivity. Buy when nat gas stabilizes or data center demand news hits.
Pullback Levels
Scale in on 10-15% dips from recent levels. Watch for ERCOT summer demand catalysts. More volatile than CEG — use wider tranches.
Risk Signal
Natural gas price collapse. ERCOT regulatory changes. Texas grid reliability concerns pushing policy against data centers. Broader utilities selloff on rate fears.
Next Catalyst
Earnings (watch calendar). Summer ERCOT demand forecasts. New data center interconnection agreements in Texas. PJM capacity auction results.
Tier 2 — High Conviction Structural
BTC Bitcoin · Scarce Money / Digital Infrastructure
15% target
The monetary system hedge. Visser's structural thesis: debt-to-GDP at 122% means aggressive tightening is systemically dangerous, forcing policymakers to tolerate inflation — BTC is the exit from a system that can no longer enforce discipline. Compares the current setup to "Micron before its 70-to-400 move." Lyn Alden's "Broken Money" thesis adds: zero terminal supply growth vs. 7% broad money growth.
Entry Trigger
Current ~$67K range. Visser says BTC is "in accumulation mode." Buy on risk-off selloffs when BTC correlates with equities temporarily. The structural thesis is independent of short-term price action. Dollar-cost average the largest position.
Pullback Levels
First tranche: current levels ($65-70K). Second tranche: $55-60K (meaningful pullback). Third tranche: $45-50K (extreme risk-off, would be Visser's "gift" entry). BTC is volatile — expect 20-30% drawdowns within uptrends.
Risk Signal
Visser notes BTC remains "tethered to SaaS via venture capital overhang" — correlation may take time to break. Regulatory crackdown. If Fed somehow achieves a Volcker-style tightening (unlikely at 122% debt-to-GDP but would kill the thesis).
Next Catalyst
Institutional adoption cycle (ETF flows). Fed policy statements confirming rate cuts are dead. Any sovereign debt crisis or YCC discussion. Halving supply dynamics (already occurred but deflationary pressure compounds).
ESLT Elbit Systems · Defense
8% target
The defense spending play. 5/6 consensus — only Leopold dissents. Zeihan: "Every military on Earth needs more missiles. Production takes years. Elbit is already there." DeBusschere: physical-world companies with hard assets benefit most from current rotation. Defense supplemental spending likely $200B+ floor.
Entry Trigger
Buy on any Iran de-escalation selloff — counterintuitive, but defense spending is structural (years of procurement), not tied to any single conflict. A de-escalation dip in defense stocks is a gift because the spending is already committed.
Pullback Levels
Scale in on 5-10% pullbacks from recent levels. ESLT is less volatile than most portfolio names. Israeli defense stocks can gap on Mideast headlines — use limit orders.
Risk Signal
Major peace deal that structurally reduces global defense budgets (low probability). Israel-specific political risk. Currency risk (shekel-denominated revenue). Defense budget sequestration in the US.
Next Catalyst
Defense supplemental vote in Congress (spring 2026). NATO member defense spending commitments. Any new conflict escalation = immediate catalyst. Quarterly earnings with backlog updates.
GLD Gold · Monetary / Inflation Hedge
7% target
The inflation and monetary debasement hedge. 5/6 consensus. Crabtree's defensive anchor (15%), Lyn's monetary thesis (10%), Zeihan's geopolitical hedge (10%). Visser prefers silver but gold still fits the commodity scarcity framework. Central bank gold buying continues structurally.
Entry Trigger
Buy on any dollar strength / real rate spike that pushes gold down temporarily. Gold tends to sell off when the dollar rallies — use that as the entry. Also buy if equities sell off hard (gold initially drops with everything in a liquidity crisis, then recovers first).
Pullback Levels
Scale in on 5-8% dips. Gold is the least volatile position in the portfolio — don't need to wait for massive pullbacks. Build steadily.
Risk Signal
Sustained real rate increase (Fed aggressively tightening AND inflation falling — the opposite of current regime). Strong dollar bull market. Risk-on euphoria reducing hedge demand.
Next Catalyst
CPI/PCE prints above expectations. Any sovereign debt stress. Central bank gold purchase announcements. De-dollarization developments. Fed confirming extended pause.
CCJ Cameco · Uranium / Nuclear
7% target
The uranium supply/demand play. AI data centers need always-on baseload power = nuclear. New reactor builds and life extensions increasing uranium demand while supply remains constrained. Cameco is the Western world's largest publicly traded uranium producer. 5/6 consensus.
Entry Trigger
Buy on uranium spot price dips or broader commodity selloffs. CCJ moves with uranium spot — when spot pulls back 10%+, that's the entry. Also buy on any nuclear policy news (new plant approvals, life extensions).
Pullback Levels
Scale in on 10-15% pullbacks. Uranium is volatile and CCJ amplifies that. Patient entry over multiple tranches is essential.
Risk Signal
Nuclear accident anywhere in the world. Major new uranium supply coming online (Kazakhstan expansion). If AI data center growth disappoints and baseload power demand forecasts are revised down.
Next Catalyst
New reactor approvals (US, Europe, Asia). Utility long-term uranium contracting cycle. DOE Strategic Petroleum Reserve equivalent for uranium. SMR (small modular reactor) deployment timelines.
Tier 3 — Catalyst-Dependent / Opportunistic
STNG Scorpio Tankers · Energy / Tankers
5% target
The energy scarcity and Hormuz disruption play. 22V (via Goehring): energy may be "the cheapest major asset class in the world." Hormuz closure affecting ~20% of global oil/LNG trade. Visser's commodity scarcity framework supports. Only 3/6 consensus — this is a geopolitical bet.
Entry Trigger
Buy on oil price spikes (tanker rates rise with oil). Buy when Hormuz tensions escalate — this is the opposite of "buy the dip." STNG goes UP when things get worse in energy markets. Also buy if tanker day rates spike above $40K/day.
Pullback Levels
Wait for oil/tanker rate pullbacks if you want a cheaper entry. If oil drops to $70-75, that's the tanker buy zone. If Hormuz de-escalates temporarily, tanker stocks dip = entry point for the structural thesis.
Risk Signal
Full Iran peace deal reopening Hormuz. Global recession crushing oil demand. New tanker supply hitting the water (long lead times = unlikely near-term).
Next Catalyst
Hormuz escalation/de-escalation headlines. OPEC decisions. Tanker day rate reports. Oil breaking above $100 (Visser's key level). Insurance rates for Hormuz transit.
TSM TSMC · AI Foundry
5% target
The irreplaceable AI semiconductor foundry. Makes the chips that power everything else in the portfolio. AVGO's capacity is "fully stretched" at TSMC. 4/6 consensus — Zeihan says zero (Taiwan risk), Crabtree says zero. This is the highest-reward, highest-risk semiconductor position.
Entry Trigger
Buy ONLY on significant pullbacks driven by Taiwan fear (not earnings weakness). Taiwan headlines create temporary selloffs in a stock with monopolistic market position. That's the entry. Also buy if Arizona fab timelines accelerate (de-risks Taiwan).
Pullback Levels
Wait for 15-20% pullbacks from recent levels. TSM is the most binary name in the portfolio. Small position size (5%) reflects the Taiwan risk — don't size up beyond that.
Risk Signal
Any Chinese military escalation around Taiwan. US-China semiconductor export controls tightening. Revenue concentration risk if hyperscaler ASIC programs diversify away from TSMC.
Next Catalyst
Earnings (watch for capacity utilization and pricing power). Arizona fab production timeline updates. Any US semiconductor policy changes. Hyperscaler order book commentary.
MELI MercadoLibre · Emerging Markets / Brazil
5% target
The Brazil/LatAm AI infrastructure hub play. Visser wrote a full research note: Brazil is "structurally advantaged" for AI — renewable energy surplus, falling real rates, mineral leverage, institutional capacity to host global compute. MELI is the LatAm digital economy proxy. 4/6 consensus.

Next Catalyst
Brazilian rate cuts. AI infrastructure investment announcements in Brazil. MELI earnings (e-commerce + fintech growth). IEEPA tariff reductions for Brazil (already coming down from 30-40% to mid-teens).

The Scaling Playbook

How to Deploy $X Into This Portfolio Over Time

01
Day 1: Establish the cash position (6%). Park it in SWVXX or short-term T-Bills. This is your pullback ammunition. Don't touch it until a VIX spike or meaningful market selloff creates the entry you're waiting for.
02
Week 1-2: Build Tier 1 core (CEG, AVGO, VST) to 50% of target weight. These are the universal positions. Don't wait for the perfect price — start building. Use limit orders 2-3% below the current ask to capture intraday volatility. If the market pulls back 5%+, accelerate to 75% of target.
03
Week 2-4: Begin Tier 2 (BTC, ESLT, GLD, CCJ) at 33% of target weight. BTC: start a recurring buy (daily or weekly). GLD: build steadily — it's your portfolio stabilizer. ESLT and CCJ: use limit orders on dips. Don't rush — these are structural positions with multi-year horizons.
04
Month 1-2: Fill Tier 1 to full weight on any pullbacks. If CEG, AVGO, or VST pull back 8-12% from your initial entry, add aggressively to bring them to full target weight. This is the Visser approach: "Buy AI buildout names on weakness — the themes haven't changed."
05
Month 2-3: Fill Tier 2 to full weight. Begin watching Tier 3 catalysts. BTC should be close to 15% through regular buys. ESLT, GLD, CCJ filled on pullbacks. Now start watching for Tier 3 entry signals — earnings, geopolitical triggers, technical breakouts.
06
Month 3+: Deploy Tier 3 opportunistically as catalysts trigger. STNG on oil spikes. MELI on EM dips. TSM on Taiwan fear selloffs. CORZ/IREN on confirmed AI hosting deals. TSLA on robotaxi milestones. ADM on agricultural disruption. These may take months to trigger — patience is the edge.
07
Ongoing: Use the Jacobson framework for hedging. When IV is elevated (VIX > 25), sell upside calls on your largest positions to generate income and fund downside protection. This lowers your cost basis while maintaining exposure. Sell CEG/AVGO covered calls at 15-20% above current price to fund put spreads on SPY.
